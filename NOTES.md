## Long-Term Progression & Transition Plan

**Phase 1: Newbie Gains (W1-W10ish — currently here)**
- Weights go up almost every session, everything is a PR
- Body learning movement patterns + building initial muscle
- Full PPL + KB schedule, 5 days/week

**Phase 2: Intermediate (W10-W30ish)**
- Weights go up every 2-3 weeks instead of every session
- PRs require more effort and planning
- Recovery weeks needed more regularly

**Phase 3: Base Established (~6-12 months)**
- Lifts settle into a range and stay there (e.g., squat hovers around 85-95/side for months)
- Adding 5lbs takes weeks of focused work
- This is where most people live forever — and that's fine

**Phase 3 options (pick one or rotate):**
- **Maintain** — same weights, 2-3 gym days/week. Strength stays. Minimal time.
- **Specialize** — pick one thing to push (squat PR, KB sport, flow mastery) while maintaining everything else.
- **Rotate** — cycle between gym phases and KB/flow phases. Month of gym, month of flows. Strength doesn't disappear in 4 weeks.

**The transition plan:**
- When PPL weights stabilize and stop PRing every other week, shift to:
  - 2 gym days (push/pull or full body)
  - 2-3 KB flow days (rotating different flows from the library)
  - 1 legs day (squat is the anchor — always keep squatting at least 1x/week)
- The whole point of building the base now = freedom to do the fun stuff later without losing anything

**The one rule:** Keep squatting at least once a week, even on light weeks. It's the most full-body compound, most technique-dependent, and most carryover to everything else. Doesn't have to be heavy — 65x8 x3 deep and slow is enough for maintenance.

## Weekly Intensity Menu

Not every week has to be a full gym week. Pick the mode that matches how you feel. Consistency over intensity — showing up light beats skipping entirely.

**🟢 Full Send** — Normal PPL + KB schedule. Weights progressing. Feeling good.

**🟡 Form Check / Recovery** — Same exercises, flat or lighter weights, stop when form breaks. Focus on quality. Do this when sore, crunchy, or coming off a big week. (W7 is an example.)

**🟠 KB Only** — No gym. OTD flow, B-stance flow, skill work (bent press, TGU, windmill), light C&P. Still a real training week, just lower impact. Good when the gym feels like too much but you still want to move.

**🟠 KB PPL** — Kettlebell versions of push/pull/legs:
- Push: C&P, overhead press, push-ups
- Pull: KB rows, swings, pull-ups at home
- Legs: goblet squats, lunges, cossack squats
Whole week with just a bell. Harder than it sounds.

**🔴 Active Recovery** — Long walks, morning flows, stretching, zone 2 cardio. No structured lifting. Good for travel weeks, sick weeks, or "I just feel off" weeks. Still counts as training.

**⚫ Rest** — Do nothing. Sleep. Eat. Sometimes this is the right call. Don't feel guilty.

The only thing that actually hurts progress is doing nothing for *weeks* at a time. Everything else is fine. The trend over a month matters, not any single week.
