# 41 Training Tips (Hannah ApexFit)

- **Source:** [X/Twitter thread](https://x.com/hannahapexfit/status/2033506191511572527)
- **Author:** Hannah ApexFit (@hannahapexfit)
- **Date captured:** 2026-03-16

## TL;DR

9 years, 2000+ hours gym experience distilled into 41 fundamentals. Core thesis: **consistency > everything else.** Form + frequency + exercise selection matter, but the priority hierarchy is clear: consistency first, then quality execution, then training frequency, exercise selection, training split. Everything else is secondary.

## Key takeaways

- **Form is the leverage point.** "How you lift matters more than what you lift." Form improvement > rep/weight chasing.
- **Hypertrophy parameters:** 9-14 working sets/workout, 3-4 exercises, take sets to 0-2 RIR, 3-4 second eccentrics, longer rest periods.
- **Machines are underrated.** Hack squats > back squats, Smith machine is good, machines excellent for hypertrophy (easier CNS demand, consistent loading).
- **Simple systems win.** Stop changing workouts. Simplicity scales. Consistent execution of boring programming beats program-hopping.
- **Separation of concerns.** Gym builds muscle, nutrition drops fat. Don't confuse the two.
- **Morning workouts as forcing function.** They improve odds of successful day structure.

## Discussion

### 2026-03-16 — First read

**Alignment with functional-bulk program:**
- We're already doing this: 4x/week frequency, ~11 working sets/day, 3-4 exercises, tracking, rest periods
- Form progression over chase: this is embedded in our structure
- The machines point is interesting — we're mostly barbell/dumbbell. Her case for Smith machine + hack squat + leg machines for controlled loading is solid for isolation work on leg days without stability demands
- Her priority list (consistency > form > frequency > exercise > split) matches what we're doing

**Where I'd nuance:**
- **#20, #21** ("Hack squat > back squat, RDL > deadlift") — These are strong claims. Both have value depending on the person, back health, goals. Context matters.
- **#37** ("High reps to shred is rubbish") — Oversimplified. High reps don't cause fat loss, but they're fine for conditioning work capacity on a cut. Calorie deficit is the real answer.
- **#38** (60 min max) — Depends on the person. Some tolerate and benefit from longer sessions. Intensity > duration.

**Worth exploring:** Could test Smith machine work or more machine-based days for leg days. The CNS preservation angle is legit — might allow more volume with better quality.

I've spent over 2000 hours in the gym over the last 9 years.

Here are 41 Training Tips I wish I knew when I first started.

🧵

1. Use the gym to build muscle and nutrition to drop fat.

2. Stop 'winging it'. With no plan, you will spin your wheels and waste months or years with little to no progress.

4. Stop changing your workouts. You cannot 'shock your muscles into growth'.

5. Simplicity scales. Simple workouts, repeated, measured, and improved over time are key.

7. How you lift matters more than what you lift.

9. Track your workouts. Without this accountability and data you will struggle to progress.

10. Use consistent rest periods. 'Guessing' your rest periods leads to inconsistency and unreliable data.

11. Use 9 - 14 working sets per workout

12. Pick 3-4 exercises per workout

13. Once form is nailed, take each set to 0-2 RIR (reps in reserve)

14. If your last reps each set aren't 'involuntarily slow' you are not working hard enough

15. Rest longer between sets. Short rest periods = worse results.

16. Use 3-4 second eccentrics (elite chance to load tension on the target muscle for hypertrophy).

17. Quality form + correct intensity + consistency is more important than training splits or exercise selection.

18. Machines are elite when it comes to hypertrophy. Do NOT avoid them.

19. The Smith machine is good not bad.

20. Hack Squats are better than back squats.

21. RDLs are better than conventional deadlifts.

22. Wrist straps on pull/back days are a must.

23. Use flat sole shoes or bear feet on leg days.

24. Put your weights away.

25. Nobody is looking at you.

26. It's you vs you. Get a little bit better consistently over time.

27. Consistency is all you do is the key to success and results:
⚬ Turning up
⚬ Rest Periods
⚬ Exercise selection
⚬ Execution

⚬ Everything

28. The best place to train is where you can be consistent.

30. The type of protein powder you choose will not influence your results.

31. You will always feel better after the gym than before.

32. Morning workouts drastically improve the chances of the rest of the day being a success.

33. Training consistently will improve every single area of your life.

34. The key to results is consistency and improving your form, reps, and weight in key lifts over time.

35. I'm convinced an elite gym playlist will increase adrenalin and performance.

36. Your training should not change whether cutting or bulking.

37. High reps to shred is rubbish.

38. Anything over 60 minutes is doing more harm than good.
