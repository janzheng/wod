## Exercises & Form Cues

### 1. Incline Dumbbell Chest Press
**Muscles:** Chest, front shoulders, triceps

### 2. Single Arm Hammer Strength Lat Row
**Muscles:** Back (Lats), Biceps

**Form tips:**
- Initiate the motion through the elbow and drive it down into crest of the hip
- Imagine your hand is a hook holding on and your elbow is pulling the hook
- Keep chest slightly tucked to keep [tension on back]

### 3. Life Fitness Tricep Dip
**Muscles:** Triceps, Chest

**Form tips:**
- Push down as far you can until your elbows lock
- Focus on resisting gravity as you lower the weight back up

### 4. Pull-ups
**Muscles:** Back (upper back), Biceps

**Form tips:**
- Drive elbows as if you're trying to drive them into your back pocket
- Control ALL the way down for 2-3s, half reps don't count

### 5. Incline Bicep Curl
**Muscles:** Biceps

**Form tips:** *Voiceover in video*

### 6. Cable Lateral Raise
**Muscles:** Side of shoulder

**Form tips:**
- Keep elbow straight or slightly bent
- Initiate motion through the elbow
- Think about driving elbow out and up

**Alternative:** Dumbbell Lateral Raise (if you workout at home)

### 7. Bulgarian Split Squat
**Muscles:** Quads, Glutes

**Form tips:**
- Controlled lowering phase and make sure to get hip even with knee at the bottom
- Controlled but powerful push up from the bottom
- Pause at the bottom of reps to get more out of less reps
- Drive knee over the toe

### 8. Romanian Deadlift
**Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings, Erectors

**Form tips:**
- Slight knee bend
- Initiate motion by pushing butt/hips back
- Push hips back (while keeping the spine neutral) until they won't go back any further
- Drive hips forward to meet the bar

### 9. Captain's Chair Leg Raise
**Muscles:** Lower abs, obliques, hip flexors

**Form tips:**
- Keep knees slightly bent and tuck your knees to the chest
- Focus on lifting your hips towards your rib cage to flex your spine
- You can do knee tucks if straight leg is too difficult for you

### 10. Machine Crunches
**Muscles:** Upper + lower abs

**Form tips:**
- Stay connected to the pad
- Focus on initiating the movement through your ABS, not pulling with your arms
- Imagine your hands are just a hook holding on & your chest is tucking down towards your hips to move the weight

## Key Takeaways

- **Elbow-driven movements** — Recurring theme across rows, lateral raises, pull-ups. The cue shifts from "moving the weight" to "moving the joint" (elbow).
- **Hip-driven movements** — RDL and BSS use the same "initiate through the hip" framing. Push hips back, drive them forward. Knees follow.
- **Full range of motion** — Pulling down fully, lowering with control, locking out. Half reps don't count.
- **Tension management** — Resisting gravity on the way down, keeping tension on target muscle (not lockout rest).
- **Hand/limb as a "hook"** — Both tricep dips and machine crunches use this cue: hands/limbs are just anchors, the actual mover is the joint (elbow down into hip, chest down to hips).
- **Pause reps** — Bulgarian split squat: pause at bottom to get more out of less reps. This increases time under tension.
- **Setup details matter** — Single-arm variations, chest positioning, unilateral emphasis (BSS, single-arm rows).

### 2026-03-16 — First read

**Alignment with what we're already doing:**
- Incline DB chest press: core movement in our push day
- Lat rows (single-arm or machine): in our pull day
- Dips: available at Arrilaga, good option
- Pull-ups: classic, we use lat pulldown as accessible alternative
- Lateral raises: staple for shoulders
- Incline bicep curls: complement to flat bench curls
- RDL: already in our day 5 legs
- Bulgarian split squat: unilateral leg work, we do DB lunges (similar pattern)
- Captain's chair leg raise: good ab option for lower abs + hip flexors
- Machine crunches: upper + lower ab focus

**Form insights that stick:**
- The "elbow as the prime mover" framing is strong. Rather than "pull the weight," it's "drive the elbow." This shifts focus from load to tension.
- "Control ALL the way down for 2-3s, half reps don't count" — aligns with the eccentric focus we have (3-4 second eccentrics from Hannah ApexFit). Explicit here.
- Resistance on the way down is the key growth stimulus. This is hypertrophy 101 but the emphasis is good.

**Program overlaps:**
- BSS: unilateral leg work, good pairing with barbell squat (which we don't do, but leg press + this would cover it)
- RDL: core posterior chain, already in our day 5 legs
- Captain's chair: good ab option, better engagement than machine crunches for some people
- Machine crunches: similar, but the "hands as hook" cue is elegant for ab isolation

**The "hands as hook" concept:**
Both tricep dips and machine crunches use this language. It's a cue to shift responsibility away from the limb and into the target muscle. Same idea as "elbow-driven" — the limb is just the attachment point, the muscle is the engine.

**Pause reps insight:**
BSS emphasizes "pause at bottom to get more out of less reps." This is time under tension strategy, which pairs with Dean Turner's eccentrics. Different angle: pause (isometric) vs. slow lower (eccentric). Both increase total tension.
