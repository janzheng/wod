# 5x Per Week Training Split for Max Gains

**Date:** 2026-03-02
**Source:** [@DeanTTraining](https://x.com/deanttraining/status/2027766145156108697)

## The Split

Two variants, both hitting 5 sessions per week with 2 off days for cardio:

### Option A
| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Pull |
| Tuesday | Push |
| Wednesday | Legs |
| Thursday | OFF (cardio) |
| Friday | Upper |
| Saturday | Legs |
| Sunday | OFF (cardio) |

### Option B
| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Upper |
| Tuesday | Legs |
| Wednesday | OFF (cardio) |
| Thursday | Pull |
| Friday | Push |
| Saturday | Legs |
| Sunday | OFF (cardio) |

## Discussion

### What's Smart About This

**Legs 2x per week is the key insight.** Quads and glutes are the biggest muscle groups and respond well to higher frequency. Hitting them twice means more growth stimulus without needing to destroy them in a single brutal session. You can spread the volume across two days — maybe a quad-dominant day and a posterior-chain day, or a heavy day and a volume day.

**Push/Pull split + a combined Upper day is clever.** The dedicated Push and Pull days let you hit chest/shoulders/triceps and back/biceps with real focus and volume. The Upper day acts as a second touch for everything — lighter, more pump-focused, filling in gaps. This gives most upper body muscles 2x frequency without needing a full PPL repeat.

**The math on frequency:**
- Legs: 2x/week (two dedicated days)
- Back/biceps: 2x/week (Pull day + Upper day)
- Chest/shoulders/triceps: 2x/week (Push day + Upper day)

That 2x frequency per muscle group per week is consistently what the research supports for hypertrophy.

### Option A vs Option B

**Option A** front-loads Pull/Push back-to-back (Mon/Tue), then rests Thursday. This means you hit upper body hard early in the week and get a break before the Friday Upper session. Downside: Push on Tuesday means shoulders/triceps are fatigued from Monday's Pull day only if there's overlap (there shouldn't be much).

**Option B** starts with a combined Upper day, then Legs, then rests Wednesday before splitting into Pull/Push. This spaces things out more evenly — you never have three consecutive training days. The Wednesday rest also means you're fresh for the Pull/Push block.

**Option B seems slightly better** for recovery. Three consecutive days (Mon-Wed in Option A) is rough, especially with Push following Pull. Option B's maximum consecutive streak is 2 days.

### What I'd Watch Out For

- **Upper day programming matters a lot.** If it's basically "Pull + Push lite," you're fine. If it turns into another heavy session, you're overreaching.
- **Leg day differentiation.** Two identical leg days is a waste. Day 1 could be squat/quad-focused, Day 2 could be hinge/hamstring/glute-focused. Or heavy vs. volume.
- **"OFF = cardio" is good framing.** Active recovery, not Netflix. Zone 2 cardio on off days supports recovery and doesn't cut into gains.

### Compared to a Standard PPL

A classic PPL 6x/week hits everything 2x but gives you zero rest days. This 5x split achieves the same frequency for all major groups while giving 2 recovery days. That's a meaningful advantage for people who have lives outside the gym, or who find that 6 straight days grinds them down.

The trade-off: your Upper day has to be efficient. You're compressing what would be a second full Push + Pull day into one session. That's fine if you're smart about exercise selection — pick one compound + one isolation per group and move on.

## Application to Current Program

The current Functional Bulk program runs a 5-day split with a different structure (Push/Pull/Legs/Upper-Lower/Full). Dean's approach could work as a rotation or next mesocycle:

**Potential mapping:**
| Dean's Day | Focus | Example Lifts |
|---|---|---|
| Pull | Back width + thickness, biceps, rear delts | Lat pulldown, cable row, face pulls, DB curls |
| Push | Chest, front/side delts, triceps | Chest press, OHP, lateral raises, tricep pushdowns |
| Legs A (quad focus) | Quads, calves | Hack squat, leg press, leg extension, calf raises |
| Upper | Full upper pump | Incline DB press, chin-ups, lateral raises, hammer curls |
| Legs B (posterior focus) | Hamstrings, glutes, calves | RDL, leg curl, hip thrust, walking lunges |

**Verdict:** Solid split. The 2x legs frequency is the biggest win. Option B has better recovery spacing. Would consider this for a future mesocycle — it's a clean framework that hits everything twice without the burnout of 6-day PPL.
