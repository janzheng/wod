# Progression Readiness — When to Move to a Harder Split

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Current week:** Week 3 of Functional Bulk Dynamic

## Status

Still getting significantly sore (DOMS) after every workout. Body is still adapting to the current volume and movement patterns. Not ready for higher-frequency splits yet.

## The Plan

- **Stay on Functional Bulk Dynamic for at least 4-8 weeks** before considering a harder split like Dean's 5x (Push/Pull/Legs/Upper/Legs).
- **Key signal to watch:** soreness becomes manageable — you feel it but it doesn't limit your next session. That means recovery capacity has caught up to the training stimulus.
- **Revisit after Week 4** (around 2026-03-17) to check in on how soreness and recovery are trending.

## Why Not Jump to 5x Now

- Adding more gym frequency (e.g. 2x legs, dedicated Upper day) when still heavily sore just digs a deeper recovery hole.
- The current 3 gym + 2 KB structure is plenty of stimulus for adaptation right now.
- KB days build work capacity and conditioning without overloading recovery — exactly what's needed early on.

## Week 4 Notes

- **Pull day: stick with machine row** — Arrillaga has a chest-supported machine row which gives the same back isolation benefit (lats, traps, rear delts, no lower back fatigue). The `incline-bench-dumbbell-row` is a good travel/home gym alternative but not needed when you have the machine. Source: [Dean Turner](https://x.com/deanttraining) recommends incline DB row specifically for gyms without a machine row.

## When Ready, Consider

- Dean's 5x split (Option B for better recovery spacing) — see `5x-weekly-split-max-gains.md`
- Or a hybrid: swap one KB day for a second leg-focused gym session while keeping one KB conditioning day
