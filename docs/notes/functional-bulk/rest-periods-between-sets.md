# Rest Periods Between Sets

**Date:** 2026-03-02
**Source:** [@DeanTTraining](https://x.com/DeanTTraining/status/2028195465502789694)

## The Argument

Rest duration vs set effectiveness (based on 15 years coaching + research):

| Rest | Set Effectiveness |
|---|---|
| 1 min | ~30% |
| 1.5 min | ~60% |
| 2 min | ~80% |
| 3 min | ~90% |
| 4 min | ~95% |
| 5+ min | ~95+% |

Key point: diminishing returns after 3 minutes. The jump from 1→2 min is massive. The jump from 3→5 min is marginal.

## Discussion

### The Science Backs This Up

The numbers roughly track phosphocreatine (PCr) recovery — your muscles' immediate energy system:
- ~50% PCr recovered at 1 min
- ~85% at 2 min
- ~95% at 3 min
- ~99% at 5 min

This is why your second set at 1-minute rest feels drastically worse, but resting 3 minutes you barely notice the fatigue.

### The Practical Sweet Spot

**For hypertrophy (our goal): 2-3 minutes is the sweet spot.**

Why not longer? Because total session volume and metabolic stress matter too. A study (Schoenfeld et al., 2016) found that 3-minute rests produced better hypertrophy than 1-minute rests — but the 3-minute group also completed more total reps. The rest itself isn't magic; it's that you can do more quality work.

Why not shorter? Dropping to 1 minute cuts your next set's output by ~70%. You're basically doing junk volume at that point — logging sets that look good on paper but aren't stimulating growth.

### What This Means for Different Exercise Types

- **Heavy compounds (squat, leg press, rows):** Rest 3 min. These are CNS-demanding and benefit most from full recovery.
- **Moderate isolation (curls, lateral raises, leg curls):** Rest 2 min. Less systemic fatigue, recover faster.
- **Light / pump work (cable flies, face pulls):** Rest 60-90 sec is fine. The goal is metabolic stress and pump, not max output.

### The Crowded Gym Factor

At Arrilaga, rest periods are often dictated by equipment availability rather than physiology. This is actually fine — if someone jumps in during your rest, you naturally get 2-3 minutes. The danger is the opposite: rushing through sets at 1-minute rest because you feel guilty hogging a machine.

**Don't rush.** That 1-minute rest set is operating at 30% effectiveness. You're better off doing 3 quality sets with proper rest than 5 rushed sets.

## Application to Functional Bulk Program

### Current approach
The program JSON doesn't specify rest periods per exercise. The timer in the app defaults to a global rest period.

### Recommended rest periods by exercise type

**Day 1 Push:**
- DB Bench / Incline DB / Shoulder Press: 2.5-3 min
- Lateral Raise / Cable Fly / Cable Lat Raise: 90 sec - 2 min
- Tricep Pushdown: 90 sec

**Day 3 Pull:**
- Lat Pulldown / Seated Cable Row: 2.5-3 min
- Face Pull / Reverse Fly: 90 sec - 2 min
- Cable Curl / Hammer Curl: 90 sec - 2 min

**Day 5 Legs:**
- Squat / RDL / Leg Press: 3 min (these are brutal, take your time)
- Calf Raise / Leg Curl: 2 min
- DB Lunge: 2-3 min (cardio-demanding)

**KB Days (2 & 4):**
- Conditioning focus — shorter rests (30-60 sec) are intentional here for metabolic training. Different goal.

## Verdict

This is one of those "simple but most people get it wrong" things. The biggest takeaway: **don't feel guilty about resting 2-3 minutes on compounds.** It's not laziness, it's how you get the most out of every set. The gym session might take 10-15 minutes longer, but the quality of work is dramatically better.
