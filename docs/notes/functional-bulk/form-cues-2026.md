# Form Cues Reference — 2026

**Purpose:** A reusable per-exercise reference for modern (2026-era) form cues. Workouts cite this doc; they don't have to re-explain every cue. Weekly workouts stay flexible — pick which cues to spotlight, adapt loads, override anything when the week calls for it.

**Source philosophy:** Modern PT, joint-by-joint, Squat-U-style biomechanics, soft-tissue-aware. NOT 2010-era bro-science. See `NOTES.md` "Program Philosophy" for the full framing.

**How to use:**
- When designing a weekly workout, copy 2-3 cues into the spotlight exercise's notes
- For non-spotlight lifts, copy the 1-2 sharpest cues only
- This doc evolves — when a cue clicks (or fails) in a real session, update the cue here
- The "Spotlight rotation" section at the bottom tracks which exercises got deep focus when

## Push exercises

### Push press

**Setup** (Jan does DB version — 40/side. Cues note both BB and DB.)
- **Feet parallel, hip-width** — NOT duck stance (W7 discovery). Duck stance = hips kick out under load.
- DBs racked on front delts, elbows up *just enough* to keep them pinned (or barbell on front delts if doing BB version). Don't chase a clean front rack — that's a different lift.
- **Ribs DOWN** before the dip — squeeze the front of the ribs toward the hips. No rib flare = no lower-back compensation.

**Movement**
- **Shallow dip** (~3-4 inches), straight down through the heels. The dip is for the legs to drive, not for momentum.
- **Vertical path** — DBs (or bar) go from front-of-shoulders straight up to *over the heels* at lockout. With DBs: two parallel lines next to your ears. Head pokes through at the top.
- If the load drifts forward, the lockout is fake (front delt finish, not push).

**Tempo + breath**
- Drive is fast, lockout is solid, descent is controlled
- Breath: in at the dip, out on the drive

**Common faults to watch for**
- Rib flare → arched lower back → QL/lumbar load (the W8/W9 fatigue pattern)
- Pressing the head back instead of moving the head through → cue: "look forward, walk the head through"
- Dipping with the knees forward instead of hips back → unstable platform

**Swap**: Seated DB shoulder press at 35-40lb if the shoulder isn't ready to stand-and-load. Use the W9D1 rotation cue: DBs facing IN at the bottom (palms toward face) → facing OUT at the top.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: 40/side hard with proper form (40x8/12/8). **Set 1 rib flare** — chest pushed out immediately on the dip, controlled for it on subsequent sets. Slight forward bar drift but not full T-shape, less drift than KB presses. Slight crackle on right shoulder by set 3 — 40 is the right working weight for the form-first cycle, don't push to 45.

### Lateral raise (DB)

**Setup**
- Stand tall, feet hip-width
- DBs at sides, slight bend in elbow ~10-15° — *fixed throughout the rep*

**Movement**
- **Lead with the elbow**, hand passive — the wrist follows the elbow
- "Pour the water out of two cups" at the top — slight internal rotation, pinky leads up by a hair
- **Stop at shoulder height.** Going higher just shrugs the traps and offloads the medial delt.

**Tempo + breath**
- 2-second eccentric on the way down — that's where the deltoid grows
- Breath: out on the lift, in on the way down

**Common faults**
- Locked elbow — stresses the elbow joint, recruits traps to compensate
- Going past shoulder height — shifts work to traps
- Body sway / momentum — dead-stop reps, no swing

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: Leading with the elbow on set 1 caused a shooting line (not super painful) from scapula down to elbow. **The fix that worked:** between sets, roll shoulders + actively engage/disengage scapula a few times. Sets 2 and 3 felt way better, set 3 was hard "in the right way." Make scapula reset between sets a default when shoulder is acting up — don't just push through it. (Stretches helping overall; the right-shoulder crackle is real but manageable.)

### Chest press machine

**Setup**
- **Seat height: handles align with mid-chest / nipple line** — NOT shoulder height. High handles = front-delt press, not chest.
- Set the **shoulder blades back AND down** into the pad before unracking — active scap retraction is the foundation
- Feet flat, slight arch in lower back is fine, no overarching

**Movement**
- Slow eccentric, **3 seconds down**
- Full stretch at the bottom but no shoulder shrug forward
- Press to **almost lockout** — leave a hair of bend, don't lock and stack on the joint

**Tempo + breath**
- 3s down, 1s pause at full stretch, drive up
- Breath: in on descent, out on press

**Common faults / watchpoints**
- **Right scap → front-pec referral** (W9D1 discovery — likely pec minor / subscapularis). If it twinges, slow tempo *first*, drop weight only if needed.
- Shoulder blades drifting forward off the pad as fatigue sets in → reset between sets
- Bouncing out of the bottom — kills the stretch stimulus

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: 165x10/10/10/6 — hit the rep target three sets, fourth set couldn't pick up after rest. Right pec super sore — directly connected to the shoulder, can be massaged out. The "maintain form vs push hard" choice is real here: 165 is the edge where form starts breaking, and the form-first call is to drop reps not technique.
- W11D1 (spotlight): 165x10 → 165x8 → 165x3 → 150x5, all stopped out. Even left side hard. **Big finding: order matters more than protocol for the spotlight.** W11 used the equipment-consolidated layout (push press → lat raise → incline DB → THEN chest press), so chest press came after 10 prior DB sets vs W10's 6. The right pec / scap protocol didn't fix it; fatigue did. **Lesson: when consolidating equipment order, place the spotlight lift earlier, not last in the warm-up trail.** For non-spotlight chest-press weeks, the consolidated late placement is fine — the tradeoff is acceptable.

### Incline dumbbell press

**Setup**
- **Bench at ~30°**, NOT 45°. Steeper bench = more front delt, less upper chest.
- **Shoulders packed back AND down** — pin the back of the shoulder into the bench, like trying to break the bench in half with your scaps
- DBs in lap, kick up to start position one at a time

**Movement (the W9D1 spotlight)**
- **Forearm stacked vertically over the elbow at the bottom** — when the DBs are at chest level, the elbow is directly under the wrist. *This* is the cue that made 40lb feel honest. The cue IS the progression, not the weight.
- **Elbows ~45° from torso** — not flared 90° (shoulder-grinder), not tucked tight (becomes tricep press)
- Press to a fingerwidth apart at the top — no DB clack

**Tempo + breath**
- **Pause 1s at the bottom** — "dead weight" before driving up
- Breath: in on descent, out on press

**Common faults**
- Forearm tilting outward at the bottom → elbow ahead of wrist → shoulder gets the load instead of the chest
- Bench too steep → drifts to shoulder press
- Head/neck tension → relax the head into the bench

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: 45x10/8/9/7 at 30° — really hard even at this load. Forearm stack is **automatic at 30°**, going wider toward T-shape feels awkward. Lower than I used to do it but it's the right angle. Lesson: **don't need 2-up-2-down tempo** at this load — the form alone makes 45 honest. Held at the bottom on set 3 instead.
- W11D1: dropped to **40lb pair (from prescribed 45)** because right chest/shoulder connected muscle was tight going in — felt fairly easy at 40, smart deload to preserve the spotlight + end block. The warmup work is paying off (shoulder warmer/less painful than W10), but on a tight day 40 is the right call.

### Kinesis / cable fly

**Setup**
- Cables at chest height (or slightly higher for upper-pec emphasis)
- Slight forward hinge at the hips — chest leads, not the head
- Stagger stance for stability if needed

**Movement**
- **Soft elbow ~10-15° bend, fixed throughout** the rep — these are NOT bicep curls, the elbow doesn't move
- Think **"hug a tree"** — the squeeze comes from the chest, not the arms pulling together
- **Stop the hands a fistwidth apart at the top** — don't crash the handles. The last few inches are arm work, not chest.
- Full stretch at the bottom — let the cables pull the arms back, get the deep pec lengthen

**Tempo + breath**
- Slow eccentric (~3 sec) for the stretch stimulus
- Breath: out on squeeze, in on stretch

**Common faults**
- Elbow angle changing through the rep → turns into a press
- Pulling past midline → arms take over, chest peaks too early
- Body too upright → upper chest only, lose the mid/lower pec

**Movement**
- **Lead with the elbow, hand passive** — wrist follows

- Fight the resistance on the way down — no snap-back

**Tempo + breath**
- Slow controlled descent
- Breath: out on the lift, in on the way down

**Common faults**

- Shoulder shrug as fatigue hits → traps take over
- Stiff elbow → wrist/forearm strain

### Incline cable overhead tricep extension

**Equipment note (Jan-specific):** Despite the "incline" name, Jan does this **kneeling on the cable column** facing away from a high pulley. The torso forward lean creates the "incline" angle without a bench. Don't write cues assuming a bench — they won't apply.

**Kinesis fallback (cable column busy):** W11D1 → Kinesis 5 (4 was too easy).

**Setup**
- Kneel facing away from the stack with rope attachment overhead
- Grip the rope behind the head
- Torso slightly forward — head between arms at the bottom (this is your "incline")

**Movement**
- **Elbows pinned**, only forearms move
- Hands shoulder-width on the rope — full long-head emphasis
- **Full stretch at the bottom** — let the load pull the forearms back behind the head
- Stop just shy of lockout at the top, slow eccentric

**Tempo + breath**
- Controlled both directions, no swing
- Breath: out on extension, in on stretch

**Common faults**
- Elbows flaring outward → shoulders/lats take over
- Locking out hard → joint stack
- Standing too upright → loses the overhead stretch angle that makes this version work

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: "went great / felt great, hard at end" — kneeling cable column version is reliable. Keep this setup.
- W11D1: Cable column busy → switched to Kinesis. **Kinesis 4 too easy, Kinesis 5x12 significantly harder.** Lock Kinesis 5 as the working number for the Kinesis fallback. Cable column kneeling remains preferred when free.

### Straight arm pulldown

**Equipment note (Jan-specific):** Done on the **cable column** — has been the setup the entire program. Working weights: W3-W8 climbed 90 → 120lb; W10 form-first prescription is ~100. (A prior version of this doc claimed a W9 switch to Kinesis — that was an AI hallucination. Kinesis is a 1-12 scale; the working numbers have always been 90-120, which only makes sense on the cable column.)

**Kinesis fallback (cable column busy):** W11D1 → Kinesis 6 ≈ cable column 100 (user calibration: "machine not linear, this feels like 100").

**Setup**
- Stand a step back from the cable column high pulley
- **Hinge slightly at the hips** — chest down ~15°, not standing fully upright
- **Slight elbow bend at the start (5°), fixed throughout** — perfectly straight arms = elbow injury risk

**Movement**
- **Pull the bar to the thighs, NOT past.** Past the thighs = shoulders rolling forward = lats off, delts on.
- **Squeeze the lats hard** at the bottom — "lats squeezing oranges into the back pockets"
- Slow controlled return to full overhead stretch — feel the lats lengthen
- Stop the rep when shoulders try to take over

**Tempo + breath**
- 2-second eccentric, 1s squeeze at the bottom
- Breath: out on the pull, in on the return

**Common faults**
- Pulling past the thighs → shoulder roll → trapezius / front delt finish
- Standing too upright → lats can't fully shorten, becomes a tricep / shoulder move
- Speeding up the eccentric → loses the lengthen stimulus

**Notes from real sessions**
- W10D1: cable column 100, 12/12/10 stopped out. "Oof really hard today." — accumulated push-day fatigue showing up here. (W7=110, W8=120, W10 pulled back to ~100 for form-first.)
- W11D1: Cable column busy → **Kinesis 6 ≈ cable column 100** (user calibration: "machine not linear, this feels like 100"). Lock Kinesis 6 as the Kinesis-fallback working number.

## Pull exercises

### Pull-up

**Setup**
- Grip slightly wider than shoulders (vary across sets — climber handles, wide, normal — the W8 pattern)
- **Active hang start** — at full extension, lats engaged, shoulders pulled DOWN away from ears. Not a dead/collapsed hang.

**Movement**
- **Pull elbows DOWN to the ribs**, NOT chin to bar. The chin clearing is a marker; the work is the lat squeeze.
- **Chest leads to bar** at the top — drive the chest forward, mid-back squeezed
- **Eyes ahead, head neutral** — no chin tucking, no neck craning
- Slow eccentric, ~2-3 seconds, return to active hang (not collapsed)

**Tempo + breath**
- 1-second pause at the top contraction
- Breath: out on pull, in on descent

**Common faults**
- Kipping / leg swing — set ends if you can't get a clean rep
- Dead-hang start (collapsed shoulders) — leads to shoulder dump and weak first inch
- Going chin-only (partial top) — chest must drive forward, otherwise it's a neck pump

**Notes from real sessions**
- Mixed grips (climber handles, wide, normal) keep it interesting and hit different lat angles (W7-W8)
- Wider grip = more lat isolation, less bicep
- Cumulative fatigue on push/pull/KB stacks affects pull-up reps more than bodyweight changes

---

### Preacher curl

**Setup**
- **Armpit pressed firmly into the pad** — not just resting. Back of upper arm flat against pad.
- Bar/handle in mid-grip, wrists neutral

**Movement**
- Full stretch at the bottom — don't lose tension by curling short of full extension
- Curl toward face, not chest
- Brief pause at peak contraction

**Tempo + breath**
- 3s eccentric — fight the long-head stretch on the way down
- Breath: out on curl, in on eccentric

**Common faults**
- Lifting elbows off the pad mid-curl (kills isolation, recruits front delt)
- Shrugging shoulders to help (form fail)
- Partial range at the bottom (most common — full extension is non-negotiable)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 15/side is the working weight (W5 finding); 20/side too heavy at 2 reps
- Incline DB curl (`incline-curl`) at 25 is the proven backup when preacher is taken (W7+) — NOT `concentration-curl` (different catalogue exercise; user has never done that variation; W7-W10 entries originally mislabeled, corrected W11D3)
- Drop set 15→10 to failure is good programming

### Dumbbell row (single arm, bench-supported)

**Setup**
- Knee on bench, opposite foot grounded; bracing arm flat on bench
- **Flat back like a tabletop**, head neutral
- DB in working hand, arm hanging fully extended

**Movement**
- **Pull elbow toward HIP, not toward armpit** — toward armpit = rear delt; toward hip = lat
- **Don't rotate the torso** — both shoulder blades parallel to floor at all times. Rotation = unilateral lat torque, fakes the weight.
- Slow descent, brief squeeze at top
- Don't slam DB at bottom — control the stretch

**Tempo + breath**
- 1s squeeze at top, 2s eccentric
- Breath: out on pull, in on eccentric

**Common faults**
- Torso rotation — looks strong, doesn't actually move the lat through full ROM
- Pulling toward armpit — works rear delt instead of lat
- Heaving with the lower back

**Notes from real sessions**
- 80x10 was a clean PR (W7-W8), W10 holds at 80 with focus on the cue
- Pyramid pattern (60→70→80) feels natural and works
- Climbing days affect grip — weight feels heavier without grip changes

### Chest-supported DB row (incline bench)

**Setup**
- Chest fully on the incline bench, no arching, head neutral
- Feet flat on floor or wedged for stability
- DBs hanging fully extended at start

**Movement**
- **Chest stays glued to the bench** — if it lifts off, the weight is too heavy
- Same elbow-to-hip cue as single-arm DB row
- Both elbows pull together, simultaneous

**Tempo + breath**
- 1s squeeze at top, 2s eccentric
- Breath: out on pull, in on eccentric

**Common faults**
- Chest coming off the bench (chasing weight)
- Head dropping or lifting (changes the spinal line)
- Asymmetric pulling (one arm leads — slow down)

**Notes from real sessions**
- Permanently replaced cable row (W7) — pulley slippage made cable row inconsistent
- 40lb felt good (W7), 45 on last set when 40 felt easy

### Lat pulldown

**Setup**
- Knees pinned firmly under pad, feet flat
- **Wide grip near the bend** — wider grip = more lat isolation, less bicep
- Slight backward lean ~15°, NO further (more = becomes a row)

**Movement**
- **Pull bar to upper chest** — never behind the neck (shoulder grinder, no benefit)
- Elbows down and back, "lats to back pockets"
- 1s pause at the bottom contraction
- Slow controlled return to full overhead stretch

**Tempo + breath**
- 1s pause at bottom, 2s return
- Breath: out on pull, in on return

**Common faults**
- Pulling with biceps instead of lats — wider grip helps reduce this
- Leaning back too far (becomes a row)
- Bar to lap (full ROM stops at chest)
- Behind-the-neck pulldowns — actively bad for shoulders, never do

**Notes from real sessions**
- 160 strict is honest working weight; 170 was too heavy for clean form (W7-W8)
- Machine variance is real at Arrilaga — different machines run different scales
- Wider grip on heavy pull-up days when biceps are torched
- **Dual pulley calibration (W11D3):** when only the dual pulley is free, **70/side ≈ 140 on the regular lat pulldown** (user: "it's like half the weight, so 70 is 140 more or less"). Use this conversion when only the dual pulley is available.

### Face pull

**Setup**
- Rope at upper-chest / face height
- Step back to set tension
- Soft athletic stance

**Movement**
- **Pull rope to face level**
- **ELBOWS HIGH (above shoulders)** — this is the make-or-break cue
- **External rotate at the top** — knuckles point UP, like a double-biceps pose
- 1s pause at the contracted position
- Slow controlled return

**Tempo + breath**
- 1s peak hold, 2s return
- Breath: out on pull, in on return

**Common faults**
- Pulling to chest (becomes a row)
- Elbows dropping below shoulders (loses rear delt focus)
- No external rotation (loses the shoulder-health benefit — this lift exists for the rotation, not the volume)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 50x20 with slow tempo is the W7-W8 stimulus (slow tempo IS the progression, don't bump weight until 3x20 is controlled)

### Reverse fly (DB, seated bent-over)

**Setup**
- **Seated bent-over** — chest toward thighs (the W3 default form)
- DBs hanging fully extended, soft elbow ~10-15°

**Movement**
- Wings out and slightly UP to shoulder height
- **Lead with the elbow, hand passive** — same pattern as lateral raise
- Slow controlled both directions

**Tempo + breath**
- 2s eccentric, 1s squeeze at peak
- Breath: out on lift, in on descent

**Common faults**
- Rounding upper back instead of hinging at hips
- Swinging the weights / using momentum
- Locking the elbow (stresses elbow joint, recruits lats)

**Notes from real sessions**
- Seated bent-over is default form (W3 discovery), beats standing
- Reverse-incline (chest-down on incline bench) is even better when bench available — removes the awkward hunch

### High cable bicep curl

**Equipment note (Jan-specific):** Setup **rotates week to week based on what's free** at Arrilaga — dual pulley (stand between two cables, two-arm) OR single high pulley (one arm at a time). Both are valid. The session log MUST record which one was used each time, because the scales are very different and not comparable across setups. Don't lock the workout description into one setup; describe the movement and let the user pick the available machine, then log it.

**Setup (dual pulley — when free)**
- Stand between two cables, arms out at shoulder level
- Cables high, handle in each hand
- **Upper arms parallel to floor, fixed throughout the rep**

**Setup (single pulley — fallback, common)**
- Single high pulley, handle in one hand
- Stand to the side of the cable, arm out at shoulder level
- Same upper-arm-parallel cue; switch sides per set or per round

**Movement (both setups)**
- Curl hand to ear, peak squeeze
- If the elbow drops, becomes a regular curl — the high arm position is the whole point

**Tempo + breath**
- Slow eccentric, no bounce at start position
- Breath: out on curl, in on eccentric

**Common faults**
- Upper arm drifting down (most common — kills the high-cable benefit)
- Bouncing at the start position (loses tension)
- Rushing the eccentric

**Working weights by setup (do NOT cross-quote across rows)**
| Setup | Logged sessions | Working number |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| Dual pulley (middle machine, hands outside the handle area) | W7 first time: 50→60 (better without full lockout) | ~60 |
| Single pulley ("back left" or whichever is free) | W8: 120x15x2 (hard) · W10D3: 100 easy → 120 a lot better/harder slow | ~120 |
| Cable column (one arm at a time) | W11D3 first time on cable column: 150x12 each side x2 (felt good, could go up; one-arm-at-a-time felt nice vs the dual-pulley lat pulldown) | ~150+ |

**Notes from real sessions**
- Without full lockout at the bottom keeps tension on biceps (W7 discovery, holds across both setups)
- 2 sets only in the finisher (trimmed from 3 in W8)
- When writing weekly workouts, give a setup-agnostic prescription ("high cable curl, find the working number on whatever pulley is free — recent: ~60 dual / ~120 single") instead of locking one

### Cable crunch

**Equipment note (Jan-specific):** Setup **rotates week to week** depending on what's free — cable column (W7-W8 workspace) OR a single-pulley regular machine (W10D3). Scales are very different and not comparable across setups; the session log MUST record which machine was used. Don't lock the workout description into one machine; describe the movement and let the user pick what's available, then log it.

**Setup**
- Kneel under a high cable (cable column OR single-pulley machine — whichever is free), rope (or strap) behind head
- **HIPS LOCKED** — this is the most important cue

**Movement**
- **Crunch from the abs only** — spine flexes, hips don't move
- Hips moving = hip flexor work, not abs
- Chest toward thighs, hold the crunch 1 second
- Slow eccentric to start position

**Tempo + breath**
- 1s peak hold, 2s eccentric
- Breath: out hard on the crunch, in on the return

**Common faults**
- Pulling with the arms (becomes a triceps move)
- Hips moving (hip flexor work, not ab work)
- Partial ROM at the top (no peak contraction)

**Working weights by setup (do NOT cross-quote across rows)**
| Setup | Logged sessions | Working number |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| Cable column | W7 first time: 50/60 too easy → 110 challenging · W8: 120x12x2 · W11D3: 120 felt a bit easy | ~120, push toward 130 |
| Single-pulley regular machine | W10D3: 57.5x15 (easy) | finding it, ~60-70 next push |

**Notes from real sessions**
- W7: core had been doing hidden work from squats and KB for 7 weeks → first time was already at 110
- 2 sets only in the finisher
- When writing weekly workouts, give a setup-agnostic prescription with both scales noted, NOT a single locked weight

## Legs exercises

### Squat (back squat)

**Setup**
- Bar on **upper traps** (high-bar position) or rear delts (low-bar — more powerlifting). High-bar is the default for this program.
- Squeeze the upper back to make a "shelf" for the bar
- Feet shoulder-width to slightly wider, toes out 10-30° to taste
- **Brace the abs like prepping for a punch** — intra-abdominal pressure, not just lower back tension

**Movement**
- **Hips back AND down**, not just down. Hips initiate the descent.
- **"Spread the floor"** with the feet — active outward rotation through the feet engages the glutes and hip abductors. Feels like screwing the feet into the ground.
- **Knees track over toes** — the 2010 myth ("knees over toes is bad") is dead. Knees following toes is normal and healthy. What matters is the line, not the position.
- Descend until thighs at parallel or lower (depth depends on mobility)
- Brief pause at the bottom (no bounce)
- **Drive through the WHOLE foot** — heel + ball + outer edge together. Not toes only, not heels only.

**Tempo + breath**
- 2s descent, brief pause, drive
- **Breath: deep belly breath at the top → HOLD through the descent (intra-abdominal pressure) → exhale on the drive up**

**Common faults**
- **Butt wink** (lumbar flexes at the bottom) — fix with mobility work, not by stopping short of depth
- Heels coming up (ankle mobility issue — work on it, or use a slight heel raise temporarily)
- Knees caving inward (glute med weakness — fix with banded squats and external rotation drills)
- Hips shooting up first ("good morning squat") — quad weakness or weight too heavy

**Notes from real sessions**
- W9D5 milestone: 55/side became a place you could "live in the bottom of" — adaptation marker
- W7-W8 hit 75-80/side cleanly, 80 is no longer a test
- Plate counting convention: weights are plates only (e.g., "65/side" = 175 total with 45lb bar)

### Incline RDL (45° hyperextension bench)

**Naming note (W11D5 correction):** prescribed as "back extension" through W10 — user flagged this as wrong. The exercise is an **incline RDL** performed on the 45° hyperextension bench. The form is a hip hinge, not a lumbar extension. Catalogue ID: `incline-rdl`. The legacy `45-degree-back-extension` ID stays for the rounded-back style, but this program uses `incline-rdl`.

**Setup**
- **Pad position: just below the hip crease** — NOT on the upper thigh, NOT on the stomach. The pad is the hinge fulcrum.

**Movement**
- **This is a HIP HINGE, not a back extension.** Think "Romanian deadlift on a machine."
- **Lower back stays NEUTRAL** throughout — neither rounded at the bottom nor hyperextended at the top
- **At the bottom:** keep neutral spine, feel the stretch in the **hamstrings and glutes**, NOT the lower back. If the lumbar rounds, you're going too deep for current mobility.
- **Drive up by squeezing the glutes and hamstrings** — these are the prime movers, NOT the spinal erectors
- **At the top:** stop at neutral. Driving past neutral = lumbar load = QL irritation. The top is a straight line from heels to head, no further.

**Tempo + breath**
- **3s eccentric, 2s pause at bottom, 1s drive up.** The slow eccentric is the entire reason this lift works.
- Breath: in on descent, out on drive

**Common faults**
- Pad on stomach or upper thigh (kills the hinge)
- Hyperextending at the top (lumbar load → QL flag)
- Rounding the lower back at the bottom (lumbar flex under load)
- Moving fast (loses the stimulus — speed is the worst progression here)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 25lb plate dead slow with 2s pause torches the hammies (W7-W8)
- Slower before heavier — go to 4s eccentric before adding weight
- Identified as hidden fatigue driver in W8 (likely contributor to QL spasm) — keep but RESPECT it
- W11D5: 25lb dead slow, all sets done with extra rest between sets, glute squeeze emphasis. User corrected the naming: "MARKED WRONG not back extensions" — this lift has always been an incline RDL in execution; the program now matches.

### Leg press

**Setup**
- Feet shoulder-width, mid-foot on platform, slight toe-out
- Back flat against the pad, head supported

**Movement**
- **Press through the WHOLE foot** — heels grounded, push the platform with heels and ball together
- Lower as deep as the hips can stay neutral (no butt wink off the pad)
- Slow descent (3s), no bounce at bottom
- Don't lock out hard at the top — leave a hair of bend

**Tempo + breath**
- 3s descent, controlled drive
- Breath: in on descent, out on drive

**Common faults**
- **Hips lifting off the pad at depth** — signals too much depth for current mobility. Back off.
- Pressing with toes only (quad-dominant, knee stress)
- Locking out hard (joint stack)
- Knees caving (same fix as squat)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 4 plates at FULL ROM is the W8 stimulus — but it's ALSO the hidden hip-stabilizer hammer (W9D5 confirmed)
- 3 plates at MEDIUM ROM is the W9 deload load — adductors and small stabilizers fire even at this load
- Long-term: alternate heavy/full-ROM weeks with lighter/medium-ROM weeks to avoid the W8 stabilizer overload pattern

### Seated leg curl

**Setup**
- Ankles UNDER the pad, knees aligned with the machine pivot
- Lap pad locked down firmly (lock it tight, not loose)
- Settings on Arrilaga machine: **5/m/0/1** (W8 corrected baseline)

**Movement**
- Curl heels toward glutes — full ROM, full squeeze
- Don't bounce out of the bottom

**Tempo + breath**
- 2s eccentric, 1s squeeze at peak
- Breath: out on curl, in on return

**Common faults**
- Bouncing out of the bottom (kills stimulus)
- Partial ROM (most common — go full)
- Torso lifting forward to help (becomes lower back work, not hamstring)
- Wrong settings → wrong baseline numbers (verify 5/m/0/1)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 110 is working hard (W8), 90 is warmup/calibration
- Baseline numbers are on settings 5/m/0/1 — don't compare to older numbers on different settings

### Single-leg calf raise (leg press)

**Setup**
- Ball of foot on platform edge, working leg
- Opposite foot resting / stabilizing on the plate or platform

**Movement**
- **Full stretch at the bottom** — let the heel drop below the platform. That's the growth signal.
- Drive to **full plantarflexion** at the top
- Brief pause at peak

**Tempo + breath**
- 2s descent, 1s squeeze at top
- Breath: continuous, don't hold

**Common faults**
- Rushing reps (no stretch, no contraction — just a wiggle)
- Partial ROM (calves love full range)
- Bouncing at the top (skips the squeeze)

**Notes from real sessions**
- 4 plates/side single leg felt solid (W7-W8)
- W9 planning note: a small plate UNDER the ball of the foot to deepen the stretch — **tested W12D5, rejected, see below.**
- W12D5 — plate-under-foot rejected. On the incline leg press the balls of the feet already sit on the bottom edge of the platform with the heels hanging off, so the heel drop gives the full stretch on its own — a plate under the foot "doesn't make sense" on this machine. The stimulus is the **slow eccentric**, not added elevation (W12D5 slow count at 3 plates was "incredibly hard"). Stay flat: balls of the feet on the platform edge, deep heel drop, 3-4s eccentric. The program ran double-leg from W11 on (single-leg + slow was the W10D5 trap).

### Dumbbell lunge (walking)

**Setup**
- Stand tall, DBs at sides, neutral spine
- Walk forward into the lunge

**Movement**
- **Long stride forward** — short stride = knee crashes forward = knee stress
- **Vertical shin on the front leg** — knee tracks toes, doesn't crash forward
- **Back knee toward floor** — 1-2" off, don't slam
- **Drive up through the front HEEL**, not the toes
- Torso vertical — no leaning forward to cheat

**Tempo + breath**
- Controlled descent, slight pause at bottom
- Breath: in on descent, out on drive

**Common faults**
- **Short stride** → knee crashes forward → knee stress
- Torso lean (compensates for weak glutes)
- Heel popping up on the front foot
- Loaded too heavy when stabilizers are fried (W8D5 lesson — 40lb at the end of a long legs day seized hip flexors / QL)

**Notes from real sessions**
- W8 found 40lb DBs were the W8 ceiling — and contributed to QL spasm. RESPECT the load order: lunges late in a session magnify everything upstream.
- W10 deloaded back to 25 — earn the way back to 40 over weeks, not in one jump
- Treat as the "QL canary" lift — abort the set if the back twinges

## Kettlebell exercises

KB Tuesday/Thursday is the **skill + mobility flank** (W10+ cycle reframe). Form is the entire progression — load is held at home-base sweet spots, not chased. See `NOTES.md` "KB weight philosophy" for the load-cap framing: 25lb for skill/flow/mobility (bent press, windmill, cossack, TGU, B-stance flow); 50lb only for the strength block (C&P, rows, swings, goblet squats), and only when the form-first ramp earns it.

### Bent press

**Setup**
- Bell racked on the bell-side shoulder, free arm hangs across the body
- Feet ~shoulder-width, slight stagger ok
- **Eye on the bell** before the lift starts — and stays on it through the whole rep

**Movement**
- **Lateral hip hinge AWAY from the bell side** — push the bell-side hip out sideways
- Knee on the bell side stays soft; the free-side leg can stay long
- **Press up by moving body DOWN, not pushing bell up** — the lift is mostly leverage. The bell barely moves; your body changes shape under it.
- **Spine stays long** — this is a *lateral hinge with overhead load*, NOT a side bend. If the lower back compresses, you're side-bending.
- Free hand can drift toward inside of front foot for counterbalance; bell-side arm stays vertical
- Slow descent back to rack, controlled

**Tempo + breath**
- Slow throughout — this is a leverage lift, not a press
- Breath: exhale on the hinge down, inhale on the return

**Common faults**
- Side-bending instead of hinging → lower back compresses → QL load
- Losing eye contact with the bell → losing the bell path
- Pushing the bell up with the arm → press collapses, leverage missed

**Swap**: Half-kneeling windmill if the standing lateral hinge is sketchy on a given day.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W3 — 25lb is the working sweet spot. 20 is easy, 30+ is the balance/tension challenge.
- W7 — "groove the path" was the cue that clicked; bell-side hip drives the lateral hinge.
- W10D2 — back to home-base 25 after 3-week gap, felt right. The lateral hinge cue is durable; doesn't need re-discovery each return.

---

### Cossack squat

**Setup**
- Feet wide, ~double shoulder-width, toes slightly out
- Goblet hold or unloaded
- **Working leg in full external rotation** — hip opens out, foot tracks the knee

**Movement**
- **Sit DEEP into the working leg** — heel stays planted
- Free leg stays long, heel down, toes can point up
- Free leg's adductor stretches as you sit deeper
- Drive up through the working heel, transition to the other side smoothly

**Tempo + breath**
- **Slow descent, find the bottom** — the depth is the work
- 2-3s descent, brief pause at the bottom, smooth ascent
- Breath: inhale on descent, exhale on drive

**Common faults**
- Heel popping up on the working leg → drop weight or shorten stance
- Knee caving inward → cue: "knee tracks foot" + ankle mobility limit
- Rushing through the bottom (the most useful position)

**Swap**: Lateral lunge with no load, or reduce stance width if depth is limited.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W3 — easy at 45 (uncalibrated load).
- W7 — 25lb produced the best depth; heavier compresses the bottom and shortens range.
- W10D2 — 25lb 8 each side, slow-and-deep produced a hard stop at end-range. **Range is the limit, not strength** at this load. Don't chase heavier — the depth IS the progression.

---

### Windmill

**Setup**
- Bell locked overhead on the bell-side, free arm hangs
- Feet wider than hip-width, bell-side foot can rotate slightly out
- **Eye tracks the bell** — same as bent press

**Movement**
- **Hip hinge AWAY from bell side** (same pattern as bent press)
- Reach the free hand toward the inside of the front foot
- **Long spine** — stretch should land in the **hip flexor of the back leg** and the hamstrings, NOT the lower back
- Bell stays vertical and locked overhead the entire time
- Return slow and controlled

**Tempo + breath**
- Slow descent, brief pause at the bottom, controlled return
- Breath: exhale on the hinge down, inhale on the return

**Common faults**
- Lower-back stretch instead of hip-flexor stretch → spine isn't long, you're side-bending
- Losing the bell vertical → arm collapses inward
- Rushing the descent → losing the hip hinge groove

**Swap**: Half-kneeling windmill, or unloaded windmill with focus on the hinge pattern.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W3-W7 — 25lb home base, occasional 30 when very fresh.
- W10D2 — 25lb felt good after 3-week gap. The hinge pattern is shared with bent press; cueing one cues the other.

---

### Turkish get-up (TGU)

**Setup**
- Lie on back, bell on the floor next to bell-side shoulder
- **Clean the bell to the rack first**, then punch up overhead — both arms
- Bell-side leg bent, foot flat; opposite leg straight
- Opposite arm at 45° to body, palm down

**Movement — checkpoint discipline**
- **Lying:** bell punched up, eyes ON the bell (eyes track the bell every checkpoint)
- **Roll to elbow:** drive through opposite heel
- **Roll to hand:** straight base arm, chest up
- **Hip bridge:** glute squeeze, ribs down (no flare)
- **Sweep leg through to lunge:** plant the back knee under the hip
- **Stand up:** drive through the front heel, bell stays vertical
- **Reverse:** same checkpoints in reverse, no rushing — the descent is half the lift

**The setup is the work** — get the bell punched to a stable **straight-arm overhead lockout** before any get-up movement. The clean-to-rack is just the brief entry step; the get-up itself runs from the locked overhead position with the arm vertical the whole way up and down — there is NO rack hold during the get-up. If the overhead lockout is wobbly, the rest of the lift inherits the wobble.

**Tempo + breath**
- Pause at every checkpoint — deliberate, not flowy
- Breath: steady throughout, full exhale at hip bridge to keep ribs down

**Common faults**
- Rushing checkpoints → bell drift → wobble compounds
- Eyes leaving the bell → bell drifts off vertical
- Going to standing too fast (the hardest transition, especially on weak side)

**Swap**: Half get-up (lying → hand, then reverse) for left-side practice without the to-standing transition. Or naked TGU (no bell) for pattern drilling.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W2 — first TGU, 15lb, left clunky. Right fluid by end of session. Coordination was the bottleneck, not weight.
- W3 — 25lb workable, left still unstable.
- W7 — 25lb x 3 each side x 2 rounds, controlled.
- W10D2 BREAKTHROUGH — **"focus on the clean itself" makes load disappear.** Bumped UP from prescribed 25lb to 40lb mid-session and 40 still felt weightless. The setup phase being the work, not the get-up itself, is the unlock. Sits next to the C&P "knee-bent catch" as a reusable cue. **Left side still harder than right (especially to-standing), but left is more *correct* because it's weaker and gets the deliberate reps.** Weak-side-first protocol producing better quality on the left.

---

### Clean & press (C&P)

**Setup**
- Bell on the floor between feet, slightly forward
- Hinge to grip the handle, opposite arm hangs across body
- **Knees bent at the catch** — this is the cue that makes everything downstream work

**Movement**
- **Hips drive the clean**, NOT the arm pulling — feel the hinge, then the snap
- **Catch the clean with knees slightly bent** — absorbs the impact, makes the press seamless (W3 discovery, the cue that made 50 click)
- At rack: bell on the chest, **elbow tucked into ribs**, wrist neutral
- Press: **ribs DOWN** (no rib flare), bicep finishes by the ear, full lockout
- Lower with control to rack — no slam
- Reset and repeat or switch sides

**Tempo + breath**
- Clean is fast/snappy, press is solid, descent is controlled
- Breath: exhale on the press, inhale at the rack reset

**Common faults**
- Rib flare on the press → arched lower back → QL/lumbar load (W8 trigger pattern)
- Catching the clean with straight legs → hard impact → press starts from a tense rack
- Pulling with the arm instead of driving with the hips → bell path gets long and loopy

**Swap**: Strict press from rack (no clean) if the clean technique is the issue. Or single-arm DB press if KB rack is unavailable.

**Notes from real sessions**
- W3 — 50lb began clicking; 3x6 each side fluid. Right ahead of left (normal).
- W6 — left-side breakthrough: shoulder moved the bell up, not the arm. The pattern the right side found in W3, the left found in W6.
- W7 — 50lb x 8 for ALL 3 sets — first time hitting 8 consistently. Lighter flow (3x3 vs 5x5) earlier in session = more energy for strength block.
- W8 — 50lb x 8 working but contributed to QL spasm pattern (combined with hip-hinge volume + lunges).
- W10D2 — re-acquaintance: 35lb x 5, then 40lb x 5, both good. **Projection: start at 40 next week, work toward 45.** The 35→40→45→50 ramp is on track for the next 2-3 KB sessions. **No 50 cold after a multi-week gap** — that's the QL-spasm-territory weight.
- **Both-sides symmetry is the goal.** Right has historically led; make left match right's *quality*, not chase right's speed.

### B-stance swing flow (compound)

The B-stance flow is a chain of movements (B-stance swing → swing clean → ab-stance goblet squat → swing snatch → overhead lunge), drilled at light load for fluidity. Treat as one unit.

**Setup**
- One foot back, **heel UP on the back foot** — most weight on the front leg
- Bell starts on the floor between feet
- 25lb is the home-base load — proven at W7 sweet spot

**Movement (universal cues across the flow)**
- **Hike pass between the legs**, NOT a squat-and-lift
- Lats packed down, bell stays close to the body
- **Hips snap forward** — bell floats to chest height on momentum, not lifted by the arms
- For the swing-clean transition: catch into rack, **soft elbow into the body** — no slam
- For the snatch lockout: bicep by the ear, **ribs down** (no flare)
- For the overhead lunge: bicep stays glued to the ear; if it drifts forward, the lockout is fake
- Vertical front shin in the lunge, long stride

**Tempo + breath**
- Smooth, not violent — the flow should feel like one continuous movement
- Breath: exhale on each hip snap / press / drive

**Common faults**
- Squat-and-lift on the swing instead of hip hinge → quads do the work, hips don't
- Bell drift on the snatch lockout → ribs flare, lower back compensates
- Front-loaded squat collapsing forward → back foot too far forward

**Notes from real sessions**
- W7-W8 — 25lb x 3 each side x 3 rounds is the sweet-spot dose. More volume = the flow stops feeling like a flow.
- W10D2 — same dose, felt great. Almost too light initially, hours later confirmed it was the right dose. Don't add load.
