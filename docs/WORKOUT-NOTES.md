# Workout Notes & Program Context

Living document tracking goals, decisions, session logs, and plans for the Functional Bulk Dynamic program.

## Goals & Aesthetic

- **Target physique:** "Offseason bodybuilder" / "powerbuilder" look -- significant muscle mass, not cutting hard, eating at maintenance or slight surplus. Looks strong and dense, not lean/vascular. Sometimes called "bear mode" or "thick/athletic" build.
- **Starting point:** ~190 lbs, wants to get leaner/fitter/more bulk
- **The path:** Train heavy compounds consistently, eat in slight surplus (~200-300 cal above maintenance), high protein (~150-190g/day at current weight), progressive overload over months/years
- **Timeline:** This is a years-long process, not months

## Diet Notes

- Fish/beef/chicken for protein, protein shakes to supplement
- Veggies/salad lunches, fruits/nuts for snacks
- Eggs and cottage cheese ("elite bulking foods")
- Pasta is good fuel for heavy squat/bench days
- Ice cream and fun foods are fine (80/20 rule)
- Aim for ~150-190g protein/day
- No extreme restriction, just consistent protein intake

## Program Structure: Functional Bulk Dynamic

**Why this structure:**
- Multi-week with fixed exercises so you can track progression
- Station-based grouping to minimize equipment switching at crowded Stanford Arrilaga gym
- PPL on gym days for size, KB circuits on off days for conditioning
- Morning flows for daily recovery/mobility

### Station-Based Gym Days

**Push (fb-w1-push):**
1. Bench: bench-press 4x6-8, incline-dumbbell-press 4x8-10
2. Dumbbells: dumbbell-shoulder-press 3x8-10, lateral-raise 3x12-15
3. Cable: cable-chest-fly 3x10-12, cable-lateral-raise 3x12-15, cable-tricep-pushdown 3x10-12

**Pull (fb-w1-pull):**
1. Pull-up bar: pull-up 3x5-8
2. Barbell: barbell-bent-over-row 3x6-8
3. Cable: lat-pulldown, seated-cable-row, face-pull, cable-bicep-curl (3 rounds)
4. Dumbbells: dumbbell-reverse-fly, hammer-curl (3 rounds)

**Legs (fb-w1-legs):**
1. Squat rack: squat 4x6-8, barbell-romanian-deadlift 4x8-10
2. Leg press: leg-press 3x10-12, leg-press-calf-raise 3x15-20
3. Leg curl: seated-leg-curl 3x10-12
4. Dumbbells: dumbbell-lunge 3x10 each

### Morning Flow Concept

- Light recovery work done in the morning before the main workout later in the day
- ~30 min on a mat, no equipment needed
- Customizable per day -- different focus based on what's sore/needed
- "Nothing should feel hard. If it does, do fewer reps."
- Doesn't interfere with main workout -- different energy systems

**Day 2 Morning Flow (fb-w1-d2-morning-flow):**
- Block 1: Mobility Flow -- cat-cow, hip flexor stretch, pigeon, squat-to-stand, downward dog
- Block 2: Core (3 rounds) -- dead bugs, bicycle crunches, bird dogs, plank
- Block 3: Bodyweight Legs (3 rounds) -- BW squats, cossack squats, glute bridges, lunges, barre wide plie pulse (Action Jacquelyn), reverse nordic curl
- Block 4: Light Pushups (2 rounds) -- push-ups, wide push-ups, slow mountain climbers
- Block 5: Cooldown -- hip flexor stretch, asian squat hold, cat-cow

## Progression Model

- Week 1 = Foundation (find working weights, learn patterns)
- Next session: start at working weights, try to hit top of rep range on every set
- When you can complete all sets at top of rep range: increase weight by 5 lbs
- That's progressive overload -- "the whole game"

## Long-Term Rotation

- 8-12 weeks PPL (build muscle)
- 4-6 weeks circuit/conditioning (cut fat, improve work capacity, give joints a break)
- Repeat
- The Functional Bulk program compresses this: PPL gym days for size + KB circuit days for conditioning in the same week
